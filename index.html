RAILMAP – The Digital Twin of U.S. Railways

Photorealistic. Federal-Ready. Built for Safety.

Mission & Goals

RAILMAP aims to modernize U.S. rail infrastructure through digital twin technology, beginning with high-priority corridors in Texas. The system supports simulation, AI-based safety, maintenance planning, and real-time coordination with emergency and engineering stakeholders.

System Architecture

The RAILMAP system consists of two primary layers: (1) a photorealistic 3D visual model collected via Skydio drones and FARO scanners, and (2) an engineering-grade subsurface utility model for excavation safety and planning. The system is deployed using NVIDIA Omniverse and adheres to open data standards.

Texas Pilot (Dallas–Houston)

The first deployment corridor covers Dallas–Houston, a strategic freight and passenger rail zone. Pilot implementation includes aerial capture, 5D modeling, predictive simulation, and stakeholder dashboard integration with support from U.S. federal programs.
